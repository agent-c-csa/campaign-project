Fighting for ALL Americans

I have experienced racism. I have experienced sexism. I want to give equal opportunities to all people - man or woman, black or white. Vote Paula for President and together, we'll make America a better place for everyone.

About Me

As a child, I experienced a racist and segregated environment as well as a family that did not support my sexuality. Throughout my early adulthood, I fought for civil rights and gender equality, and I became my city’s mayor at age 26. I went on to become a state senator for two terms at age 37, and at age 50 I became a state representative, holding the position for 6 years. At 60 I became my state’s governor and retired after two terms at age 68.

Throughout my life, I have believed in equality of opportunity for all people. I favor a smaller wage gap and reduced economic inequality along with universal and free healthcare. Although I was retired, recent events have inspired me to come out of retirement and run for president.

Coronavirus Response

If I were president, I would have administered a far more decisive and strict response than President Trump. I would have immediately taken extensive measures as soon as the World Health Organization reported that coronavirus was a global risk. Also, foreseeing the effects of a lockdown on the economy, I would have set up more conditions to cushion the downfall as much as possible. Finally, I would have done more to support those unemployed during the crisis.

Health Care

I staunchly believe that universal health care is a human right, and that greedy corporations should not monopolize such a vital service. As President, I would take extensive measures to reallocate tax funds to make health care free and readily accessible.

Education

I believe that a good education is vital for every citizen of the United States, and as such I believe that as a country we must raise our standards of excellence and overall increase state funds for education through national grants-in-aid.

Climate Change

I fully understand the implications of climate change, and I firmly believe that we as a country must do our best to counter it. As President, I would do my best to limit carbon emissions through executive orders and legislation in Congress and by incentivizing citizens to engage in more energy-efficient activities.
