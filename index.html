About Me

As a child, I experienced a racist and segregated environment as well as a family that did not support my sexuality. Throughout my early adulthood, I fought for civil rights and gender equality, and I became my city’s mayor at age 26. I went on to become a state senator for two terms at age 37, and at age 50 I became a state representative, holding the position for 6 years. At 60 I became my state’s governor and retired after two terms at age 68.

Throughout my life, I have believed in equality of opportunity for all people. I favor a smaller wage gap and reduced economic inequality along with universal and free healthcare. Although I was retired, recent events have inspired me to come out of retirement and run for president.
